# Case Study: Customer Complaint Tokenization System

## 1. Case Study Title

**Tokenizing Customer Complaints for Automated Support Analysis**

## 2. Business Background

An e-commerce company receives thousands of customer messages every day through:

* Email
* Chat support
* Mobile application
* Website complaint forms
* Social media

The messages are written in natural language and may contain complaints related to delivery, payment, product quality, refund, replacement, or customer service.

The company wants to process these messages automatically. Before applying text classification or sentiment analysis, the messages must first be broken into smaller units called **tokens**.

## 3. Business Problem

Customer-support agents currently read every complaint manually.

This creates several problems:

* Complaints are not processed quickly.
* Important words may be missed.
* Similar complaints are handled inconsistently.
* Urgent complaints may not be prioritized.
* Manual analysis becomes difficult when message volume increases.

The company wants to build a tokenization component that converts customer messages into individual words and sentences.

## 4. Objective

The objective is to create a text-processing system that can:

* Read customer complaint messages.
* Divide paragraphs into sentences.
* Divide sentences into words.
* Handle punctuation correctly.
* Convert text into a consistent format.
* Preserve important business terms.
* Prepare tokens for later NLP processing.

## 6. Expected Tokenization Types

The system must support the following tokenization methods.

### Sentence Tokenization

Sentence tokenization divides a paragraph into individual sentences.

Example input:

```text
The product is damaged. Please replace it immediately.
```

Expected structure:

```text
Sentence 1: The product is damaged.
Sentence 2: Please replace it immediately.
```

### Word Tokenization

Word tokenization divides a sentence into individual words or symbols.

Example input:

```text
My order arrived late.
```

Expected structure:

```text
My
order
arrived
late
.
```

### Character Tokenization

Character tokenization divides text into individual characters.

Example input:

```text
late
```

Expected structure:

```text
l
a
t
e
```

### Subword Tokenization

Subword tokenization divides complex or unknown words into smaller meaningful parts.

Example:

```text
unacceptable
```

Possible subword structure:

```text
un
accept
able
```

## 7. Functional Requirements

The tokenization system should:

1. Accept a customer complaint as input.
2. Remove unnecessary spaces.
3. Convert text to lowercase when required.
4. Separate sentences using punctuation marks.
5. Separate words using spaces and punctuation.
6. Retain important numbers such as order IDs and payment amounts.
7. Handle contractions such as:

   * cannot
   * didn’t
   * hasn’t
8. Handle special characters such as:

   * ₹
   * %
   * *
   * /
9. Handle abbreviations such as:

   * Mr.
   * Dr.
   * ETA
10. Handle product codes and order numbers such as:

* ORD-45891
* PROD-1005

11. Preserve meaningful business phrases where necessary.
12. Generate tokens in a structured format.

## 8. Important Tokenization Challenges

### Challenge 1: Punctuation

Input:

```text
The product is damaged!
```

The system must decide whether the exclamation mark should be treated as a separate token.

### Challenge 2: Currency Values

Input:

```text
I paid ₹2,500 for the product.
```

The system must preserve the currency value correctly.

### Challenge 3: Order Numbers

Input:

```text
My order ORD-45891 is delayed.
```

The system should avoid unnecessarily separating the order number.

### Challenge 4: Contractions

Input:

```text
I haven't received my refund.
```

The system must decide whether to treat:

```text
haven't
```

as one token or divide it into:

```text
have
n't
```

### Challenge 5: Multiple Sentences

Input:

```text
The package arrived late. The product was also damaged. I need a replacement.
```

The system must identify all three sentences correctly.

### Challenge 6: Mixed Language

Input:

```text
My order abhi tak deliver nahi hua.
```

The system must handle English and transliterated Hindi words.

### Challenge 7: Emojis

Input:

```text
Very disappointed with the service 😡
```

The system must decide whether the emoji should be preserved as a token.

### Challenge 8: URLs and Email Addresses

Input:

```text
I sent the details to support@example.com.
```

The email address should not be broken incorrectly.
